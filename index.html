🥐 Croissant

A croissant is a buttery, flaky pastry made from a laminated yeast dough, in which layers of butter are folded into the dough to create its light, crisp texture. Recognizable by its crescent shape and golden-brown crust, the croissant is enjoyed around the world as a breakfast favorite or a snack. It can be served plain or filled with ingredients such as chocolate, almond cream, cheese, or ham.

Although the croissant is closely associated with France, its origins can be traced back to Austrian pastries such as the kipferl. The modern croissant was developed in France during the 19th century, where bakers adapted the traditional Austrian recipe using laminated dough. Over time, it became one of the most iconic symbols of French baking and remains a beloved pastry enjoyed in cafés and bakeries worldwide.
